Sisig

Description

Pork sisig is a popular and savory Filipino dish known for its bold and intense flavors. It is typically made from finely chopped and crispy pork, often including various parts of the pig like the ears, cheeks, and belly. These pork bits are typically seasoned with a blend of spices, including soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and chili peppers, which infuse the dish with a mouthwatering combination of tanginess, spiciness, and umami. To prepare pork sisig, the meat is first boiled, grilled, or fried until it becomes tender and crispy. It is then mixed with the flavorful seasonings and served hot, often garnished with toppings such as chopped onions, fresh cilantro, and a sunny-side-up egg. Pork sisig is a favorite among Filipinos and is commonly enjoyed as a main dish, appetizer, or accompaniment to alcoholic beverages. Its irresistible combination of textures and tastes makes it a delightful and satisfying treat for those who appreciate bold and hearty Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams pig's mask or pork belly (trim excess fat if preferred), boiled in water with black peppercorns, bay leaf, onion and garlic until tender, drained
  • as needed fish sauce and black pepper
  • as needed canola oil
  • 1 pc red onion, chopped
  • 1 (1-inch) pc ginger, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chicken liver, chopped (optional)
  • 1 pc green chilli, sliced
  • as needed bird's eye chilli, sliced
  • as needed Knorr Liquid Seasoning
  • 5 tbsps Lady's Choice real mayonnaise
  • 3 pcs calamansi

Procedure

  1. Season boiled meats then grill or cook in a 375F oven until crispy. Chop into small cubes.
  2. In pan, sauté onion and ginger. Add chicken liver and sauté until cooked. Add the chopped meats, and chilies. Season with Knorr Liquid Seasoning according to taste.
  3. Off the heat, add Lady’s Choice Real Mayonnaise and mix well. Serve hot with calamansi.
