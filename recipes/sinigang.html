Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a traditional Filipino sour soup known for its delightful combination of savory and tangy flavors. It is typically made with a variety of ingredients, including pork, shrimp, fish, or vegetables, which are simmered in a sour broth. The sourness in sinigang comes from tamarind, calamansi, or other souring agents like tomatoes or green mangoes. Additional ingredients often include leafy greens, radishes, and long green beans, which add depth and texture to the dish. Sinigang is a beloved comfort food in the Philippines, enjoyed in various regional variations, and it is often served with rice as a hearty and satisfying meal.

Procedure

  1. Wash tamarind pods under cold, running water to remove any grit or dirt from the skins.
  2. Wash tamarind pods under cold, running water to remove any grit or dirt from the skins.
  3. Using a fork, mash the tamarinds to release the pulp.
  4. Place the tamarind and liquid in a fine-mesh strainer set over a bowl. Continue to mash with a fork, returning some of the liquid into the strainer once or twice to fully extract the juice.
  5. Discard seeds and skins. Pour tamarind juice into the pot.
